## B. PER-CLAIM EXPOSURE (prior art · inventive step · §1(2) excluded matter)

**Claim 1 (C — finalisation) — STRONGEST. Lead with it.**
- *Prior art:* quorum/finality protocols and validator weight-decay both exist individually. The
  novelty is the **specific combination**: symmetric cross-dimension retention + `basis = max(total
  effective, quorum floor)`. No surveyed system solves liveness-halt AND eclipse-shrink with one
  denominator construct.
- *Inventive step:* strong. The description spells out the *sequence of failed obvious fixes* (decay
  one dimension → capital drift; decay all → halt; measure against present → eclipse) and shows the
  hybrid basis as the non-obvious resolution. This "each obvious fix reintroduces the prior problem"
  narrative is the best inventive-step argument in the whole set — it pre-empts "obvious combination."
- *§1(2):* lowest risk. Reads unambiguously as a distributed-systems / network-liveness method.
- *Verdict:* defensible. This is why it's Claim 1.

**Claim 8 (B — temporal-novelty valuation) — WEAKEST on inventive step. Honest flag.**
- *Prior art:* "novel in our corpus" but **not cleared vs MEV/commit-reveal and near-duplicate /
  novelty-detection / dedup literature** (our audit explicitly says this is uncleared).
- *Inventive step:* this is the claim an examiner attacks hardest. Rodney's own examiner-sim said
  "once a canonical order exists, assigning value to first-appearing elements would be obvious." The
  defence is NOT the novelty count alone — it is **strategyproofness against three named attacks
  (duplication, subset-padding, collusive recombination) by construction**, plus the integration with
  commit-reveal authorship ordering. Keep the claim specific (first-to-cover over a *commit-reveal*
  canonical order); do NOT broaden to "scoring novelty."
- *§1(2):* MEDIUM risk — "assigning a value to a contribution" is the limitation most exposed to a
  "mathematical/administrative method" objection. Mitigation: the value gates a slashable on-ledger
  consequence, not a mere score; lean on that in prosecution.
- *Verdict:* keep, but expect amendment; the dependent claims (10 learned-quality floor, 11
  similarity floor) are the fallback positions if the independent is narrowed.

**Claim 13 (A — non-transferable standing) — MIDDLE.**
- *Prior art:* SourceCred (non-transferable Cred / transferable Grain) and DeSoc soulbound tokens
  establish the non-transferable-vs-transferable split — but **neither makes non-transferable standing
  the CONSENSUS WEIGHT with a separate transferable state-capacity unit**. That two-part
  differentiator is the inventive core; the claim already recites it (clauses (c)+(d)).
- *Inventive step:* moderate. Strengthen by leaning on the **state-transition validity rule** as the
  enforcement mechanism (clause (c)) — that is the technical, not merely administrative, character.
- *§1(2):* MEDIUM. "Consensus franchise that cannot be sold" can be cast as a governance rule;
  defence is that it is enforced by a protocol-level validity rule that changes how the ledger reaches
  consensus, not by policy.
- *Verdict:* keep; cite SourceCred + DeSoc proactively in the spec's related-work as closest art.

**Claim 20 (combination) — the safety net.** Even if 1/8/13 each get narrowed, the specific
tying-together within one unit-and-script ledger is held by none of the surveyed art. This is our
own audit's named "strongest position." Make sure it survives any amendment to the independents.

## C. FORMAL / CLARITY DEFECTS (an examiner will raise these)

1. **[MED] Multiple independent claims, same category (1, 8, 13 all methods).** UK practice is more
   permissive than the EPO here, but expect a unity-of-invention / plurality objection. The linking
   special technical feature is the **contribution-ledger architecture** recited in claim 20; be
   ready to argue unity on that, or fall back to divisionals (one family each). *Decision for Rodney/
   attorney: file all three now and defend unity, or split.*
2. **[MED] Claim 6 wording vs Claim 1(d).** Claim 1(d) finalises on the *aggregate* sum of effective
   weights; claim 6 adds a *per-dimension* supermajority requirement. As written ("reach the
   supermajority fraction in each said dimension") it is a narrowing condition but the basis for the
   per-dimension test is unstated. **Recommend reword:** "wherein finalising further requires that,
   for each said dimension, the supporting effective weight attributable to that dimension is at least
   the supermajority fraction of that dimension's contribution to the finalisation basis." Flag for
   Rodney — claim semantics are his/attorney's call.
3. **[LOW] Antecedent basis / defined terms.** "content elements", "coverage function", "heartbeat",
   "consensus dimensions", "contributor identifier" are used without claim-internal definition. All
   are defined in the description, which is normally sufficient, but an examiner may request that the
   independent claims recite enough to be self-contained. Cheap to fix at claims-finalisation.
4. **[LOW] "verified contribution" (claim 13(a)) is a bare antecedent.** Consider "a contribution
   verified by the method of claim 8" in a dependent, to tie the families and reinforce unity.
5. **[LOW] Means/function drift.** Claims are method-form throughout (good for UK). The system claim
   18 references "instructions… cause the node to perform" — clean. No 112(f)-style means-plus-function
   exposure.
