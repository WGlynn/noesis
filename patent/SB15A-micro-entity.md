# CERTIFICATION OF MICRO ENTITY STATUS (GROSS INCOME BASIS) — content for USPTO form PTO/SB/15A

> This captures what form PTO/SB/15A asks you to certify. At filing, use the official fillable
> SB/15A PDF from uspto.gov and check these boxes / sign there; this is the content, not a
> substitute for the official form. NOT legal advice. Confirm each statement is true for you
> before certifying — a false micro-entity certification is a serious matter.

**Application / petition:** Petition for Foreign Filing License (37 CFR 5.13), inventor William Glynn.
**Title:** Method and system for earned, non-transferable consensus standing on a distributed contribution ledger, with strategyproof valuation and eclipse-resistant weighted finalisation.

The undersigned certifies that the following statements are true:

1. **Small entity.** I qualify as a small entity as defined in 37 CFR 1.27 (an inventor/individual, not under an obligation to assign to a non-small entity).

2. **Application-count limit.** Neither I, nor any joint inventor, has been named as an inventor on more than four previously filed U.S. patent applications, other than:
   - provisional applications,
   - international (PCT) applications for which the basic national fee was not paid, and
   - applications assigned to a previous employer due to an obligation of assignment.
   *(Confirm: you have fewer than five such prior US non-provisional filings. If unsure, list them and check the count.)*

3. **Gross income limit.** Neither my gross income, nor that of any joint inventor, in the calendar year preceding the year this fee is paid, exceeded the "Maximum Qualifying Gross Income" — three times the median household income reported by the Bureau of the Census for the most recent year. *(Confirm against the current USPTO-published threshold for 2026.)*

4. **No assignment to a non-qualifying entity.** I have not assigned, granted, or conveyed, and am not under an obligation to assign, grant, or convey, a license or other ownership interest in the application/invention to an entity whose gross income exceeded that Maximum Qualifying Gross Income in the preceding calendar year. *(Note: if you assign any interest to Rodney or a company, that assignee must ALSO meet the income test, or micro-entity status is lost and you drop to small-entity — $94 instead of $47.)*

**Signature:** ______________________________  **Date:** June 12, 2026
**Name:** William Glynn
**Capacity:** Inventor

> Practical note: micro-entity ($47) requires both the income test AND the <5-prior-applications
> test AND no assignment to a high-income entity. If any is uncertain, certify small-entity ($94)
> instead — still cheap, and removes the risk of a defective micro-entity certification.
