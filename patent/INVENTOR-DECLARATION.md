## PART 3 — Entity status (fee discount)

Micro-entity status (37 CFR 1.29) gives the largest fee discount. You likely qualify if all hold: (a) you qualify as a small entity; (b) you are named on fewer than five prior non-provisional U.S. applications (provisionals and foreign filings do not count); (c) your gross income in the preceding year did not exceed the USPTO's "maximum qualifying gross income" threshold (about three times the U.S. median household income); and (d) you have not assigned, and are not obligated to assign, rights to an entity exceeding that income threshold.

If you intend to assign to an entity (or to grant Rodney rights), that assignee must also meet the micro-entity income test, or you drop to small-entity status. Confirm before certifying — a false micro-entity certification is a serious problem. File form SB/15A (gross-income basis) with the application if claiming micro-entity.

## PART 4 — Rodney's role: assignee/representative, NOT inventor

Inventorship and ownership are different things. Inventorship is a question of who conceived the claimed invention and is a legal fact that cannot be granted as a favor; on this record, that is you alone. Ownership can be transferred by assignment. Rodney can be:

- an **assignee** (in whole or in part) of the patent rights, via a written assignment; and/or
- the **applicant** and correspondent / "invention representative" who files and prosecutes,

without ever being listed as an inventor. Listing a non-inventor as an inventor (or omitting a true inventor) can render the patent unenforceable, so keep the inventor field to you and handle Rodney's interest through assignment.

Skeleton assignment clause (for counsel to paper properly):

> For good and valuable consideration, the receipt of which is acknowledged, William Glynn (Assignor) hereby assigns to [Rodney's full legal name / entity] (Assignee) an undivided [___]% interest in the invention titled above, in the provisional application filed [date / serial no.], and in any patents issuing therefrom, together with the right to file, prosecute, and enforce. [Terms, consideration, and any conditions to be set by the parties and counsel.]

Recommendation: agree the split and the terms in writing before you file or share, and have counsel paper the assignment. A handshake on "the future of crypto economics" is exactly the thing that should be on paper first.

## PART 5 — What actually gets filed for the provisional

1. The specification (the v2 provisional spec).
2. At least one drawing where necessary to understand the invention (the figures page).
3. The provisional cover sheet (SB/16) with the fields in Part 1.
4. The micro-entity or small-entity certification (SB/15A) if claiming the discount.
5. The filing fee.

No claims are required in a provisional, but including the claim set (as in the v2 spec) is good practice because it disciplines the disclosure to support what you will later claim. File through USPTO Patent Center. The priority date is the filing date; you then have 12 months to file the non-provisional claiming benefit of this provisional.
